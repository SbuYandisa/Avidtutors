Computer Science

First Year

Unlocking the world of code:
The first year of the course is designed to give you a solid foundation in the fundamentals of computer science and programming. You will also learn about the underlying principles of computer science, including computer architecture, operating systems, networks, databases, and algorithms depending on the institution.


Second Year

Building on the basics:
In the second year, you will build on the foundations laid in the first year, and learn how to apply your knowledge to solve real-world problems. You will learn about the theory and practice of software engineering, and how to design and build large software systems. You will also learn about the theory of computation, and how to analyse the efficiency of algorithms.


Third Year

In the third year, you will learn about the theory and practice of artificial intelligence, and how to design and build intelligent systems. You will also learn about the theory and practice of computer security, and how to design and build secure systems. You will also have the opportunity to study a range of optional modules, including advanced topics in computer science, and topics from other disciplines.
